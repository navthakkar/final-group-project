Adrian Oh

Adrian Oh is an artist and art archivist whose main area of interest is in documenting visual resources and artist materials. Through the use of descriptive metadata, cataloging practices, and digitization methods, he aims to work with artist collections, archives, and non-book media to further contextualize artists and their work. He is most interested in the documentation processes that go behind cataloging singular items such as artists' sketches, notes, correspondence, and estate records. In his free time, Adrian also records and documents longplays of live-service games for documentation and preservation purposes. He received his BFA in Studio Art at New York University and will receive his MSLIS from Pratt Institute in 2024.

His responsibilities for the project include recording and outlining the research process for the project as a whole. He wrote the page "About the Project" on the website. He also assisted in the questionnaire development for the interview portion of the project, and conducted an interview with a public librarian for use on the "Interview" page of the website. He performed several usability tests including accessibility testing, digital rights, and content acquisition. He then wrote the portions of the Libby/SimplyE/Hoopla usability test pages on the website that pertain to those tests.

Maddy Casey

Maddy Casey is a MsLIS student (expected Spring 2025) at the Pratt Institute. She received a Bachelor of Arts degree from Gettysburg College with dual majors in French and International Affairs in Spring 2021, where she worked in the school’s Library at the Reference Desk as a Peer Research Mentor and Circulation desk Student Employee. She is passionate about the ways in which digital tools can help improve people’s access to and enjoyment of information and hopes to build a career as a Reference Librarian upon graduation. In particular, she is interested in helping patrons develop technoliteracy; building their comfort using, understanding, and discussing digital technology. While not formerly a use of ebooks for pleasure reading, this project has encouraged her to seek out and explore her local library’s ebook offerings!

Her responsibilities for the project included coding and creating the graphics for the website homepage and coding it, conducting usability testing tasks in coordination with other group members, analysis of the applications’ functionality for non-English speaking users, analysis of the e-reader applications’ User Interface aesthetics and converting the project’s Reference Librarian Interviews into content.

Navya Thakkar

Navya Thakkar is a graduate student currently studying Information Experience Design (MSIXD) at the Pratt Institute. She is an artist, scientist and designer, with a deep interest in interdisciplinary design and communications. She is passionate about designing accessible and experiential products and interfaces for users, with a focus on non-profit work. She earned her dual BA degrees in Ecology, Evolution, and Organismal Biology and Studio Art from Vanderbilt University. She is also the recipient of the Vanderbilt University Hamblet Award 2022, a travel and art grant that will culminate in a solo exhibition opening in January 2024.

Her responsibilities for the project included designing and coding the rest of the website, and assisted in the questionnaire development and testing for the interview portion of the project. Additionally, she performed usability tests in coordination with other group members to analyze the UX design of each application, and tested their use on multiple different devices.
